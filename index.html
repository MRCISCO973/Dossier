How this works
Two independent models run entirely on this phone: an Elo-style rating system that updates after every match result you import, and a goal-scoring rate model (a simplified Dixon-Coles Poisson approach) that estimates each team's attacking and defensive strength. Their predictions are averaged into the probability you see on the Match screen.
World Cup adjustments
Host nations (USA, Mexico, Canada for 2026) get a modest home advantage applied; all other matchups are treated as neutral venue. Knockout-stage moneylines settle on the 90-minutes-plus- stoppage result — if it goes to extra time, the draw outcome wins that bet regardless of who advances. The "To Advance" estimate shown separately accounts for extra time and a 50/50 penalty shootout assumption.
What this can't see
Squad news — injuries, suspensions, rotation, fatigue — isn't reflected anywhere in this model. National teams also play far fewer matches per year than club sides, so ratings here are noisier and slower to update than a club-league model would be. Treat every number on this screen as a starting point for your own judgment, not a verdict.
About the confidence score
The confidence score isn't the same as the win probability — a match can show a lopsided probability and still score low on confidence, if it's backed by little data or the two underlying models disagree with each other. It factors in how lopsided the probability is, whether the rating model and goal-scoring model agree, how much historical data exists for both teams, and (for live matches) how much time is left to play. The "strongest live read" on the Live tab is chosen by this confidence score, not simply by whichever match has the single highest percentage — a near-certain result built on thin data is treated as less trustworthy than a clear edge backed by solid data and model agreement. This is meant to help you weigh a read, not to tell you what to do with it.
About Vega
Vega is a fictional character with a paper portfolio, built for entertainment. She never places a real bet, never touches real money, and has no connection to any sportsbook, bank account, or payment method — there isn't one anywhere in this app. When switched on, she logs a paper "pick" whenever a live match's confidence score crosses the threshold you set, sized by that confidence, and settles it automatically once the match ends. Her running bankroll and record are a what-if scoreboard, not a forecast of how real betting would perform — past paper results don't predict future ones any more than the underlying model guarantees an edge against real bookmaker lines.
About MLB home run suggestions
Switch the Live tab to "MLB Home Runs" to rank that day's batters by a combined read on power, ballpark, and weather. No API key — MLB's own stats API and a free weather service (Open-Meteo) are used directly. The roughest part of this model, stated plainly: "who they're facing" uses the opposing probable starting pitcher's overall home-run rate, not real batter-vs-this-pitcher history — that level of matchup detail (platoon splits, pitch-type vulnerability) isn't available for free anywhere. Park orientation used in the wind calculation is an approximation, not a surveyed value. Treat the ranking as a starting point for your own research, the same as everything else in this app.
Not financial advice
This app is a statistical reference tool. It is not a guarantee, a recommendation, or a substitute for your own research. Bookmaker odds already incorporate most public information; a model disagreeing with the market is not the same as the model being right.
